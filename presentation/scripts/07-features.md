## Script

Here's what the platform actually does. I won't read every tile — let me land on the ones that matter, and the rest show it's a real product, not a demo.

The four that define GIX:

- **Verifiable execution** — every job is hardware-attested and checked on-chain.
- **A neutral spot market** — a real order-book price for compute, not a vendor's list price.
- **Pay-per-inference** — you escrow per job; no idle reservations, no committed capacity.
- **A drop-in, OpenAI-compatible API** — you point your existing client at one endpoint and the crypto disappears.

And then the supporting set that makes it usable end to end:

- a **tamper-evident audit** trail you can replay from Sui and Walrus;
- it's **permissionless** — anyone can provide or consume;
- it's **Sui-settled**, so sub-second finality and parallel jobs;
- a built-in **SUI→USDC on-ramp** so users can fund a purchase without leaving the app, with no DEEP token required;
- a **crypto-pairs exchange** to swap into any of our dollars — USDC, DBUSDC, or MOCK_USDC;
- real **wallet integration** showing your actual address and balances;
- and a **two-machine demo** proving a buyer and a seller on different machines, with a real on-chain buy.

The theme across all of it: **verifiable, neutral, pay-per-use.**

> **Transition:** "Let me show you the surfaces people actually touch."
